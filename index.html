Who I am

About Me

A multidisciplinary researcher and builder with roots in chemistry, biology, and technology.

I'm Kushaal — a freshman at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, originally from Hyderabad, India. My academic journey has been anything but conventional: I've worked in organic chemistry labs, built data tools for research teams, designed 3D-printable lab instruments, and analyzed cancer cell data from real animal clinical trials.

What drives me is a passion for bridging the gap between scientific research and practical technology. I believe the most interesting problems live at the intersections — between biology and computation, between lab work and software, between ideas and tangible prototypes.

I'm a published co-author in the Tetrahedron journal and have a second paper currently under review. I've interned across four different research labs and organizations before starting university, building a depth of experience rarely seen at the undergraduate level.
