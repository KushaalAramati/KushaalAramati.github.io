About

I'm an undergraduate at the University of Wisconsin–Madison who likes being where research and software meet. Over the past few years I've worked across wet-lab chemistry, single-cell bioinformatics, data-mining automation, AI prototyping, and 3D printing — and stayed financially self-sustaining through hospitality and catering work along the way.

The common thread is taking messy research needs and turning them into structured data, usable tools, and clear outputs. Right now that means QGuide — ranking CRISPR guide-RNAs by on-target performance, off-target risk, and predicted editing outcomes.

I co-authored a published synthesis paper, have a second paper under review, and hold three international karate degrees from Japan.
