About

I'm an undergraduate at the University of Wisconsin–Madison who likes being where research and software meet. Over the past few years I've worked across wet-lab chemistry, single-cell bioinformatics, data-mining automation, AI prototyping, and resin/filament 3D printing.

The common thread: taking messy research needs and turning them into structured data, usable tools, and clear outputs. Right now that means QGuide — ranking CRISPR guide-RNAs by on-target performance, off-target risk, and predicted editing outcomes.

I'm a co-author on a Tetrahedron publication and a second paper currently in review.
